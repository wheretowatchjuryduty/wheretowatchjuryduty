Why Netflix Doesn’t (and Might Not) Have Gotham Season 4:

Several factors contribute to the absence of Gotham Season 4 (and potentially other seasons) on Netflix:

  • Licensing Agreements: The most significant reason is the expiration of licensing agreements between Warner Bros. Discovery (the parent company of Warner Bros. Television) and Netflix. Netflix may have held the rights for a certain period, but these rights have likely reverted to Warner Bros. Discovery, or been licensed to a different platform.

  • Warner Bros. Discovery’s Streaming Strategy: Warner Bros. Discovery has its own streaming service, HBO Max (now Max), which is a primary destination for its content. It’s strategically advantageous for them to keep their flagship shows on their own platform to drive subscriptions and maximize revenue. This often leads to the removal of shows from other services.

  • Regional Variations: Licensing agreements vary significantly by region. A show might be available on Netflix in one country but not another due to different deals struck with local distributors. This geographical fragmentation makes it nearly impossible to provide a universal answer to where to find Gotham Season 4.

  • Demand and Popularity: While Gotham enjoyed considerable popularity, its streaming availability might be affected by the overall demand and licensing costs. Streaming services prioritize acquiring content that they believe will attract and retain subscribers.
